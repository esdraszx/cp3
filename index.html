What you can borrow to buy a house is usually 5 times your annual income. However, it can vary depending on your current debt to income ratio. Your credit score determines how low or high the interest rate is. The rate that you get can also be determined if you are a first time home buyer. If you are a first time home buyer you are able to apply for special credit opportunities. If not, you can apply for normal morgages. Apartments and townhomes are usually more affordable than houses. However, apartments and townhouses usually come with fees like HOA, but you might save some money on exterior repairs. The maximun amount that you can afford is usually determined by how much the bank will be allowing you to borrow.

Based on this information. In order to get a loan you will need to get your credit score ran by a bank or morgage lender. After, you will have to provide W-2 for the past two years to confirm income. Tax returns from the last two years to confirm income and credit history. Bank statements for the past two months to confirm history about current income/debt ratio. Last paycheck for the past month (last two if paycheck is by-weekly) to confirm current employment and income. Form of ID, and copy of Social Security Number. After the bank, they will let you know how much you can afford and at what rate. After that, you will have to start shooping for homes and it is usually advice to find a realtor to help speed the process up.
